5.4 Approvals Pathway

Site Plan Control, Minor Variance, and Rezoning Requirements for 4-Unit Conversion in R1a Zone

Site Plan Control

Site Plan Approval is NOT required for converting a single-family lot to a 4-unit building under Bill 23 [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.6, PLN].

The exemption is absolute: "residential development on a lot containing ten or less dwelling units and any buildings accessory thereto is an exemption for which there are no exceptions. Therefore, this form of development is not subject to any of the regulations of Section 9.0" [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.6, PLN].

Bill 23 modified the definition of "Development" to "exclude residential development on a lot if the lot contains no more than ten residential units, thereby exempting all aspects of Site Plan Control for residential development of up to ten units on a lot" [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.2, PLN].

Exception: Site Plan Control would still apply if the property is "Adjacent to or within a Core Area(s)" as defined (key natural heritage features, key hydrologic features, local natural areas and their vegetation protection zones), though this exemption for 10 units or less has been removed from Core Area requirements [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.6-7, PLN].

Minor Variance

The Committee of Adjustment is authorized to "authorize consents, variances to the Zoning By-law, an Interim Control By-law, extensions or enlargement of non-conforming uses provided they maintain the intent and purpose of this Plan and any other requirements of the Planning Act" [UHOP-Vol1-ChapterF-Implementation, p.15, PLN; RHOP-Vol1-ChapterF-Implementation, p.15, PLN].

Minor variances would be required if the 4-unit proposal cannot meet standard R1a zone performance standards (setbacks, lot coverage, parking, height, etc.).

Rezoning Requirements

Rezoning from R1a to a higher density zone (likely RM2 or RM3) would be required as the R1a zone typically permits only low-density residential uses (single-detached, semi-detached dwellings).

This strategy relies on asserting the provincial 3-unit-per-lot right at Committee of Adjustment.
