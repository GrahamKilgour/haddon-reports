5.2 Official Plan

Official Plan Designation and Policies for Stoney Creek/Ward 10

Official Plan Framework

Stoney Creek/Ward 10 is governed by Chapter B.7 - Stoney Creek Secondary Plans within the Urban Hamilton Official Plan (UHOP). The area contains seven distinct secondary plan areas [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.1, PLN]:

  1. Western Development Area Secondary Plan (B.7.1)
  2. Old Town Secondary Plan (B.7.2)
  3. Urban Lakeshore Area Secondary Plan (B.7.3)
  4. Fruitland-Winona Secondary Plan (B.7.4)
  5. Nash Neighbourhood Secondary Plan (B.7.5)
  6. West Mountain (Heritage Green) Secondary Plan (B.7.6)
  7. Trinity West Secondary Plan (B.7.7)

Land Use Designations

The secondary plans establish varied land use designations including:

  • Residential: Low Density Residential 1, 2, 2a, 2b, 3, 3c; Medium Density Residential 2, 3; High Density Residential 1 [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.1-2, PLN]
  • Commercial/Mixed-Use: Local Commercial, Mixed Use - Medium Density, District Commercial [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.2, PLN]
  • Other: Institutional, Business Park, Parks/Open Space, Utility [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.2, 13, PLN]

Density ranges vary by designation. For example: - Low Density Residential 2b: 1-29 units/net residential hectare [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.1, PLN] - Medium Density Residential 3: 50-99 units/net residential hectare [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.2, PLN]

Urban Intensification Status

YES - This is an urban intensification area, specifically within established neighbourhoods:

The Old Town Secondary Plan explicitly states: "All development within the Old Town shall protect and maintain the economic viability of Stoney Creek's downtown and support its revitalization through conversion, infilling and redevelopment" [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.5, PLN].

Further, intensification policies require that "Residential intensification within these areas shall enhance and be compatible with the scale and character of the existing residential neighbourhood in accordance with Section B.2.4 – Residential Intensification" [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.22, PLN].

The Fruitland-Winona area specifically targets greenfield density of minimum 70 persons and jobs per hectare to meet overall density targets [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.22, PLN].

Mixed-Use Medium Density corridors permit heights up to 12 storeys in certain locations along King Street and Highway 8 [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.9, 11, PLN].

5.4 Approvals Pathway

Site Plan Control, Minor Variance, and Rezoning Requirements for Converting R1 Lot to 6-Unit Building

Site Plan Control

Exempt from Site Plan Approval. Under Bill 23 amendments to Section 41 of the Planning Act, residential development of up to 10 dwelling units on a lot is completely exempt from Site Plan Control [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.4-6].

Specifically: - Bill 23 modified the definition of "development" to exclude "residential development on a lot if the lot contains no more than ten residential units" [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.2] - Hamilton's amended Site Plan Control By-law implements this exemption with "no exceptions" [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.6] - The exemption applies to all aspects of Site Plan Control, including site layout, grading, drainage, landscaping, and building design [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.2]

Exceptions that could still trigger Site Plan Control: 1. Buildings within or adjacent to Core Areas (natural heritage features) - though this exemption was also removed for residential developments of 10 units or less [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.6-7] 2. Properties requiring septic tanks in Source Protection Vulnerable Area 1 [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.8]

Rezoning Requirements

Rezoning required. The R1 (Low Density Residential) Zone does not permit 6-unit buildings. Based on Hamilton zoning patterns in the provided documents:

  • R1 zones typically permit single detached dwellings [ZoningBylaw05-200-Exceptions, p.620-621]
  • Multi-unit buildings (6 units) would require rezoning to a Multiple Residential zone (RM2 or RM3) or Medium Density Residential zone (R6)
  • Standard rezoning application process applies under the Planning Act

Requires verification: Specific permitted uses in the R1 zone and appropriate target zone for 6 units

Minor Variance

Likely required in conjunction with rezoning. The Committee of Adjustment is authorized to grant variances to zoning by-law requirements [UHOP/RHOP-Vol1-ChapterF-Implementation, p.15]:

  • Council appoints a Committee of Adjustment to "authorize consents, variances to the Zoning By-law... provided they maintain the intent and purpose of this Plan and any other requirements of the Planning Act" [UHOP/RHOP-Vol1-ChapterF-Implementation, p.15]
  • Minor variances would address any site-specific deviations from the new zone's regulations (setbacks, lot coverage, parking, etc.)

Requires verification: Specific dimensional requirements of target residential zone and existing lot characteristics to identify variance needs

Process Gaps Created by Bill 23

Hamilton staff noted significant deficiencies from removing 10-unit-or-less developments from Site Plan Control, particularly regarding [PED23043-ResZonesProject, p.8]: - Site grading - Landscaping - Servicing connections - Right-of-way works and land dedication - Access permits - Street tree plantings and road widenings (now addressed "at the back end" through consent/condominium applications)

5.5 Parking Analysis

Parking Requirements for 6-Unit Residential Building in Hamilton R1 Zone

Standard Parking Requirements

For a 6-unit residential building in Hamilton's R1 (Low Density Residential) zone, the building would be classified as a "Multiple Dwelling" since it contains 5 or more dwelling units.

According to the standard parking requirements:

In all other areas (outside Parking Rate Areas): - Resident parking: 1 space per unit = 6 spaces - Visitor parking: 0.3 spaces per unit = 1.8 spaces (rounds to 2 visitor spaces) - Total required: 8 parking spaces

[ZoningBylaw05-200-Sec5-Parking, p.21, PLN]

Available Parking Reductions

Hamilton's bylaw provides significant parking reductions based on Parking Rate Areas (PRA):

PRA 1 (Most Reduced):
  • Resident parking: 0 spaces required
  • Visitor parking: 2 visitor spaces + 0.05 spaces per unit = 2.3 spaces (rounds to 3 total)
  • Total: 3 spaces (62.5% reduction)
PRA 2 (Moderate):
  • Resident parking: 0.5 spaces per unit = 3 spaces
  • Visitor parking: 0.15 spaces per unit = 0.9 spaces (rounds to 1 space)
  • Total: 4 spaces (50% reduction)
PRA 3 (Minimal):
  • Resident parking: 0.85 spaces per unit = 5.1 spaces (rounds to 6 spaces)
  • Visitor parking: 0.25 spaces per unit = 1.5 spaces (rounds to 2 spaces)
  • Total: 8 spaces (minimal reduction)

[ZoningBylaw05-200-Sec5-Parking, p.20-21, PLN]

Requires verification: - Whether the specific R1 property is located within PRA 1, PRA 2, or PRA 3 boundaries - Site-specific mapping to confirm applicable Parking Rate Area designation - Any site-specific exceptions that may apply to the property

5.6 Ontario Building Code (Part 9)

Ontario Building Code Part 9 Requirements for 2-Storey Semi-Detached

Fire Separation

Party Wall Requirements

The party wall separating semi-detached units must be constructed as a fire separation with not less than 1-hour fire-resistance rating [OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.775; OBC-Part9-Alternate, p.72]. Specifically:

  • The wall must provide continuous protection from the top of the footings to the underside of the roof deck [OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.775]
  • Any space between the top of the wall and the roof deck must be tightly filled with mineral wool or noncombustible material [OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.775]
  • Note: A party wall on a property line for residential occupancy need not be constructed as a firewall if it meets these fire separation requirements [OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.775]
Penetrations and Openings

Fire separations must maintain continuity. The 2024 OBC clarifies requirements through Articles 9.10.9.6 through 9.10.9.9 for penetrations by piping, outlet boxes, raceways, sprinklers, and fire dampers to ensure the fire-resistance rating is maintained [OBC-2024-DivB-Part9-UpdateDeck, p.18].

Accessibility

Part 3, Section 3.8 contains requirements for buildings required to be accessible to persons with physical or sensory disabilities [OBC-2024-Volume11, p.12; OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.12].

Requires verification: Specific accessibility requirements for 2-storey semi-detached dwellings under Part 9.

Energy Efficiency

Part 12 regulates mandatory and enabling energy efficiency requirements and insulation in residential buildings [OBC-2024-Volume11, p.13; OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.13]. The 2024 OBC is based on the 2015 NECB with Ontario-specific variations in energy efficiency [OBC-2024-Volume11, p.9; OBC-Part9-HousingSmallBuildings, p.9].

Requires verification: Specific R-values, window performance ratings, and energy efficiency metrics for 2-storey semi-detached construction.

6.5 Four Tests for Minor Variance

Under Section 45(1) of the Planning Act, all minor variances must satisfy four tests:

Test Requirement Assessment Criteria
1. Official Plan Intent Variance maintains OP intent Aligns with intensification policies, UHOP growth targets
2. Zoning By-Law Intent Variance maintains ZBL intent Building mass, setbacks, coverage within zone character
3. Desirable Development Development is desirable and appropriate Compatible with neighbourhood, good planning principles
4. Truly Minor Variance is minor in nature <25% variance = minor; >40% = unlikely minor (Hamilton staff guideline)
