3. Neighbourhood & Location

Stoney Creek is located in Ward 10 of the City of Hamilton.

Suburban community along the QEW corridor with mix of old town heritage and new suburban development. Growing rental supply with mid-rise projects near Highland Road and arterial nodes. Highway access and retail amenities drive demand.

Stoney Creek Neighbourhood Characteristics

Parks and Open Space

Stoney Creek's planning emphasizes connected park systems integrated with natural features:

  • Park Classifications: Neighbourhood Parks serve ~5,000 people within walking distance; Community Parks (4-7 hectares) serve ~20,000 residents and multiple neighbourhoods [Hamilton-ParksMasterPlan-2023, p.20]
  • Provision Standard: Hamilton targets 2.1 hectares/1,000 people for municipal parkland (0.7 ha/1,000 each for Neighbourhood, Community, and City-wide parks) [Hamilton-ParksMasterPlan-2023, p.20]
  • Key Features: Plans include the Eramosa Karst Conservation Area, neighbourhood parks, and the East Mountain Trail Loop with multi-use paths linking natural open spaces [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.91]
  • Notable Sites: Battlefield House and Park in Old Town [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.5]; Fifty Point Conservation Area in Urban Lakeshore [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.13]

Schools and Institutional Uses

Institutional facilities are located within or adjacent to communities they serve, accommodating health, education, recreation, social/cultural activities [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.19]. Specific school locations require verification.

Transit and Transportation

  • Transit-Supportive Development: Higher densities concentrated along arterial roads serving as future transit corridors [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.92]
  • Transit Station Areas: Nearby Centennial Neighbourhoods features planned Light Rail Transit stops and GO bus/rail station at Centennial Parkway North/Goderich Road, targeting minimum 160 residents and jobs per hectare [UHOP-Vol2-ChapterB6-SecondaryPlans, p.173]
  • Inter-modal Hub: Planned transportation hub on CN Railway line at Fifty Road for bus, train, and car-pooling [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.18]
  • Barrier-Free Design: Transit facilities, sidewalks, crossings designed for persons with disabilities, seniors, children, and reduced mobility [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.92]

Walkability and Cycling Infrastructure

  • Street Design: Pedestrian-friendly streets with appealing streetscapes, barrier-free pathways, adequate lighting [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.92]
  • Cycling Network: Facilities provided per City Cycling Master Plan and Trails Master Plan recommendations [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.92, p.102]
  • Trail Connections: Non-vehicular trail network linking destinations including parks, conservation areas, residential and mixed-use areas [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.52, p.102]
  • Modified Grid Pattern: Street networks designed to maximize connectivity with alternate vehicular and pedestrian routes, streets at pedestrian scale [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.53]

Commercial Amenities

  • Mixed-Use Areas: Gathering places serving as community focal points accessible by automobile, transit, and active transportation [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.91]
  • Neighbourhood Commercial: Small-scale areas serving local communities plus larger commercial serving daily/weekly needs [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.19]
  • Regional Shopping: Centennial Node (Eastgate Square Mall area) serves regional retail function and acts as planned Light Rail Transit terminus [UHOP-Vol2-ChapterB6-SecondaryPlans, p.169]
  • Farmers Market: Public open space provision for farmers markets [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.19]

Major Employers

  • McMaster University and McMaster Medical Centre designated as Major Activity Centre [UHOP-Vol1-ChapterE-UrbanSystems, p.12]
  • Mohawk College and St. Joseph's Hospital (mountain campus) as Major Activity Centre [UHOP-Vol1-ChapterE-UrbanSystems, p.12]

Requires verification: Specific major employers within Stoney Creek boundaries; detailed cycling route maps; exact school locations and capacities.

Neighbourhood Character

  • Natural Heritage: Protection of Niagara Escarpment, environmentally significant areas, valley lands (Felker's Creek), woodlands, wetlands [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.18, p.52]
  • Cultural Heritage: Conservation of historically/architecturally significant buildings, archaeological resources [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.92]
  • Compact Urban Form: Higher densities near arterials, lower densities maintaining Escarpment views, accommodating ~9,600 people in Nash Neighbourhood (3,600 units) [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.52]
  • Housing Mix: Full range of low, medium, high-density housing supporting residents throughout life cycles [UHOP-StoneyCreek-SecondaryPlan, p.19, p.52]
