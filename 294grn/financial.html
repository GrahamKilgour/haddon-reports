9. Incentive Eligibility

Development Charges

Development Charges for a 6-Unit Residential Building in Hamilton

Applicable DC Rates

Requires verification: Specific per-unit DC rates for apartment/multi-residential units, as the context does not provide the residential rate tables referenced on "Page 2" of the rate pamphlets.

The following charges apply to a 6-unit residential building:

  • City of Hamilton DCs (By-law 24-072 as amended) [DevelopmentCharges-RatePamphlet-Dec2024, p.1, FIN]
  • GO Transit DCs (By-law 11-174 as amended) [DevelopmentCharges-RatePamphlet-Dec2024, p.1, FIN]
  • Education DCs (HWDSB Public and HWCDSB Catholic school boards) [DevelopmentCharges-RatePamphlet-Dec2024, p.1, FIN]
  • Special Area Charges (if applicable to location) [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.3, FIN]

Payment Timing: DCs are payable at building permit issuance by the City of Hamilton Building Department [DevelopmentCharges-RatePamphlet-Dec2024, p.1, FIN].

Current DC Exemptions and Deferrals

Rental Housing Exemptions (DC Act)

If the 6-unit building is rental housing, bedroom-based reductions apply: - 3+ bedroom units: 25% reduction - 2 bedroom units: 20% reduction - All other units: 15% reduction

[DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.3, FIN]

Non-Profit Housing

100% exemption if the development qualifies as non-profit housing [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.3, FIN].

Downtown Hamilton CIPA Exemption

40% exemption for residential development: - Period: June 1, 2024 to May 31, 2025 (reducing to 35% for June 1, 2025 to May 31, 2026) - Requirements: Must be within Downtown Hamilton CIPA boundaries (By-law 21-163) and meet building height restrictions per Schedule F – Figure 1 of Zoning By-law 05-200

[DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.3, FIN; DevelopmentCharges-RatePamphlet-Jun2025, p.4, FIN]

Payment Deferrals Available
  1. Rental Housing: 6 annual instalments commencing at occupancy [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.1, FIN]
  2. Apartment Developments: Subject to availability, contact Finance staff [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.1, FIN]
  3. ERASE Redevelopment Grant Program (ERG): If approved for an ERASE grant, DC deferrals are available [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.1, FIN]
Rate Lock-In Option

If a Site Plan Application or Site-specific Zoning Amendment is filed (on/after January 1, 2020), DC rates can be locked based on application date if building permit is issued within 2 years/18 months of approval. Interest charges apply [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.1, FIN].

Important Limitations
  • Exemptions and reductions DO NOT apply to Education DCs and Special Area Charges [DevelopmentCharges-FullRates, p.3, FIN]
  • Downtown CIPA exemption does not apply to Education DCs [DevelopmentCharges-RatePamphlet-Dec2024, p.5, FIN]

Requires verification: Site-specific location to determine applicable sewer system area (Combined vs. Separated Urban Area A/B) and eligibility for CIPA exemption.

Federal & Provincial Programs

HAF Programs, CMHC MLI Select Benefits, and Federal/Provincial Incentive Programs for a 6-Unit Purpose-Built Rental in Hamilton

Hamilton Housing Accelerator Fund (HAF) Programs

1. Additional Dwelling Unit and Multi-Plex Housing Incentive Program

Eligibility: Directly applicable to 6-unit projects [HamiltonHousingAcceleration, p.1]

Available incentives: - Building Permit Fee Rebate: Grant to rebate City application fee (excluding HST) for each building permit, maximum $2,000 per building permit [HamiltonHousingAcceleration, p.1] - Forgivable Loan: 15-year forgivable loan of $25,000 per eligible unit for units meeting affordability parameters, maximum $150,000 per site (6 units × $25,000) [HamiltonHousingAcceleration, p.1]

Affordability requirement: Rents cannot exceed 100% of CMHC Average Market Rent (AMR) for Hamilton during the 15-year affordability period [HousingAccelerationIncentive, p.6]

Geographic restriction: Must be within Housing for Hamilton Community Improvement Project Area (HHCIPA), excluding Sub Area 2 – Roxborough [HamiltonHousingAcceleration, p.1]

2. Housing Acceleration Incentive Program

Not applicable: This program requires "seven (7) or more net new housing units" [HousingAccelerationIncentive, p.2]. A 6-unit project does not qualify.

CMHC MLI Select Benefits

Eligibility: Fully eligible - "Minimum project size of 5 units" for standard rental housing [CMHC-MLISelect-ProductOverview, p.1]

Benefits structure: Requires minimum 50 points through commitments to affordability, energy efficiency, and/or accessibility [CMHC-MLISelect-Program, p.1]

Financial advantages: - Higher loan-to-value ratios: Up to 95% LTV (vs. standard 85% maximum) [CMHC-MLISelect-FeesAndPremiums, p.2] - Increased amortization periods - Lower debt coverage ratio requirements - Reduced insurance premiums: 6.75% premium at 90% LTV for construction financing (vs. standard products unavailable above 85% LTV) [CMHC-MLISelect-FeesAndPremiums, p.2]

Non-residential component limit: Cannot exceed 30% of gross floor area or 30% of total lending value [CMHC-MLISelect-Overview, p.1]

Federal GST/HST Rebate Programs

Purpose-Built Rental Housing (PBRH) Enhanced Rebate

Eligibility: Fully eligible - "Building has 4+ units" [CRA-GST-HST-NRRP-Rebate-RC4231, p.7]

Critical qualifying conditions (ALL must be met): - Minimum 4 units with private kitchen/bath/living area - At least 90% of units held for exempt residential leases - Construction began after September 13, 2023 and before 2031 - Substantially completed before 2036 [CRA-GST-HST-NRRP-Rebate-RC4231, p.7]

Value: - 100% federal HST rebate (5%) with no FMV cap - 100% Ontario provincial HST rebate (8%) - Total effective HST savings: ~$45,500 per unit on a $350K build [CRA-GST-HST-PBRH-Rebate, p.7]

Critical advantage: "Zero effective HST" vs. standard NRRP which has FMV phase-out thresholds [CRA-GST-HST-PBRH-Rebate, p.7]

Requires verification: Confirmation that construction start date meets the post-September 13, 2023 requirement

Standard NRRP (if PBRH ineligible)

If construction started before September 14, 2023: - Federal NRRP: 36% rebate of federal portion (5% GST) - Ontario NRRP: 75% rebate of provincial portion (8%), maximum $24,000 per unit, no FMV cap [CRA-GST-HST-NRRP-Rebate-RC4231, p.7]

CMHC Construction Financing Programs

Apartment Construction Loan Program (ACLP)

Eligibility: Requires standard apartment properties; excludes retirement, long-term care, student housing, single room occupancy, and supportive housing [CMHC-ACLP-HighlightSheet, p.2]

Minimum affordability requirement (choose one): - Criteria A: Minimum 20% of units at rents ≤ 30% of median household income for Hamilton market - Criteria B: Approval under other federal/provincial/municipal affordable housing programs providing capital grants, concessions, or expedited processing [CMHC-ACLP-HighlightSheet, p.2]

Affordability period: Minimum 10 years from first occupancy [CMHC-ACLP-HighlightSheet, p.2]

Additional requirements: - Energy efficiency: 15% improvement over 2015 NBC (Part 9 low-rise) or 2017 NECB (Part 3) - Accessibility: Minimum 10% of units meeting local accessibility standards or 2015 NBC requirements; barrier-free common areas [CMHC-ACLP-HighlightSheet, p.2]

Affordable Housing Fund (AHF)

Eligibility: Minimum 5 units for standard rental [CMHC-AHF-HighlightSheet, p.2]

Funding structure: - Repayable loans and forgivable loans (preferred option) - Contribution-only stream for projects with >75% funding secured
